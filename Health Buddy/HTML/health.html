2.SQUATS

Squat is a foundational strength exercise where you lower your hips from a standing position as if sitting in a chair, then return to standing, strengthening your legs (quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes) and core.[10 x 2]

3.Lunges

The lunge is a body resistance exercise that works the leg muscles. Specifically, the lunge targets the quadriceps and the hamstring muscles in the thigh, the gluteal muscles in the buttock, and to a lesser extent, the lower leg muscles.[5 x 2]

4.PUSH-UPS

A push-up is a fundamental bodyweight exercise that builds strength in the chest, arms (triceps), and shoulders (deltoids) and engages the core, back, and legs for stability.[5 x 2]

5.PULL-UPS

A push-up is a fundamental bodyweight exercise that builds strength in the chest, arms (triceps), and shoulders (deltoids) and engages the core, back, and legs for stability.[5 x 2]

6.PLANK

How to Do a Plank the Right WayA plank can refer to a long, flat piece of wood or a core strengthening exercise where you hold a position similar to a push-up, balancing on your toes and forearms while keeping your body in a straight line from head to heels[10 min]

7. CRUNCHES

Crunches are a common abdominal exercise performed by lying on your back and curling your upper body forward to work the rectus abdominis (the "six-pack" muscle) and other abdominal muscles.[10 x 2]

8.Leg-Raise

A leg raise is a common exercise where you lift your legs (keeping them straight) while lying on your back, engaging your core and hip flexors to strengthen and tone your abdominal muscles, improve posture, and enhance circulation.[5 x 2]
