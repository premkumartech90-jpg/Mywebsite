2.Barbell or EZ-Bar Curls

Barbell curls are a popular exercise for building bicep strength and size. To perform a barbell curl, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, grasp the barbell with an underhand grip, and curl the weight up towards your shoulders while keeping your elbows close to your body.[10 x 2]

3.Dumbbell Hammer Curls

Dumbbell hammer curls are a variation of the traditional bicep curl that targets the brachialis and brachioradialis muscles in addition to the biceps. To perform a dumbbell hammer curl, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, hold a dumbbell in each hand with your palms facing each other, and curl the weights up towards your shoulders while keeping your elbows close to your body.[10 x 2]

4.Concentration Curls

Concentration curls are an isolation exercise that targets the biceps. To perform a concentration curl, sit on a bench with your legs spread, hold a dumbbell in one hand, and rest your elbow on the inside of your thigh. Curl the weight towards your shoulder while keeping your upper arm stationary.[10 x 2]

5.Preacher Curls

Preacher curls are an isolation exercise that targets the biceps. To perform a preacher curl, sit on a preacher bench with your arms resting on the pad, hold a barbell or dumbbells with an underhand grip, and curl the weight towards your shoulders while keeping your upper arms stationary. [10 x 2]

6.Chin-Ups

Chin-ups are a bodyweight resistance exercise where you hang from a bar with an underhand (supinated) grip and pull your body upward until your chin is level with the bar

7. Cable Curls

Cable Curl is a strength-training exercise targeting your biceps for improved size and power. By pulling a cable towards your body using arm movement, it boosts upper body strength and muscle definition.
